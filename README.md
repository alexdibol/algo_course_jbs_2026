# Algo Systems — Self-Teaching Formation in Algorithmic Trading

This repository hosts the landing page for **Algo Systems**, a self-teaching course in algorithmic trading designed as a structured formation rather than a loose collection of materials. The course is built to help learners progress from foundational discipline to strategy design and finally to a deeper understanding of markets as the environment in which trading systems must actually operate.

The architecture of the course is organized around **three core pillars** and a **cross-cutting methodological layer** that bridges research and implementation through AI-assisted but governance-first workflows.

## Core Course Architecture

### Pillar I — Foundations of Algorithmic Trading Systems
Focused on system formation, reproducibility, testing discipline, execution realism, and the logic of building defensible trading frameworks.

### Pillar II — Strategy Architecture and Trading Logic
Focused on strategy design, signal construction, portfolio logic, and the disciplined translation of trading ideas into structured systems.

### Pillar III — Markets for Traders
Focused on market structure, execution conditions, institutional frictions, and the broader ecology in which trading systems succeed or fail.

### Cross-Pillar Methodological Layer — Research to Implementation Under Governance
In addition to the three pillars, the course now includes a methodological book and a corresponding collection of Google Colab notebooks devoted to one of the most important modern problems in systematic trading: how to move from research to implementation without losing transparency, accountability, interpretability, or institutional defensibility.

This layer is not a fourth pillar in the same thematic sense as the others. Rather, it acts as a **bridge across pillars**. It introduces the use of AI and agentic workflows to help transform research artifacts into implementation-ready systems while preserving governance at every stage. Its purpose is to make explicit the transition from idea formation, research design, and exploratory notebook work toward structured implementation workflows that remain reviewable, auditable, and human-accountable.

The methodological contribution therefore sits across the full course architecture. It connects foundational system discipline, strategy design, and market realism with a governed implementation logic suited to serious modern algorithmic trading work.

## Course Philosophy

Algo Systems is designed around the idea that algorithmic trading should be learned as an institutional discipline. That means the emphasis is not only on models or signals, but also on structure, testing, market realism, reproducibility, and human accountability. The course is meant to support serious independent study through a clear and navigable architecture.

The addition of the methodological book and its accompanying notebooks reinforces this philosophy. The objective is not simply to generate more code or automate strategy construction, but to show how modern AI can be used responsibly to bridge the gap between research and implementation under conditions of governance, transparency, accountability, and defensibility.
