"A strategy does not deserve trust because it belongs to a familiar category. It deserves trust only if the mechanism underlying it can be made structurally legible and if that mechanism survives perturbation." — From the Course Introduction

Most algorithmic trading education fails in one of three ways. It is too theoretical to become executable. Too technical to become conceptually meaningful. Or too promotional to be intellectually trustworthy.

This course was built to resolve that fragmentation. It treats a trading system not as a collection of signals and indicators, but as a complete engineering discipline — from data governance through strategy design to market mechanics and systemic stress.

Three pillars. Thirty-two chapters. Full books. Notebooks. Video lectures. All free. No institutional backing required.

§ 01

What Makes This Different

First Principle
Mechanism Before Prediction

Most courses obsess over signal quality while treating execution, costs, and regime change as afterthoughts. This course inverts that hierarchy. Understanding why a strategy works — and under what structural conditions it fails — is the prerequisite for trusting it at all.

Second Principle
Survival Before Optimization

A strategy that cannot survive a liquidity squeeze, a correlation compression, or a funding crisis is not a strategy — it is a bet wearing a costume. Every chapter in this course asks: what is this system implicitly short, and what happens when that position is called in?

Third Principle
Governance Inside the Code

Causal integrity, reproducible experiments, auditable artifacts, and explicit assumptions are not bureaucratic add-ons. They are part of model validity. A system that cannot be reproduced, audited, and stress-tested is epistemically fragile — regardless of its backtest metrics.

§ 04

Who This Is For

1
The Quant Who Needs More Than Math

You can build models but your backtests keep looking better than your live results. This course teaches the systematic causes of that gap: leakage, execution naivety, cost blindness, and the structural fragility hidden inside apparently clean research.

2
The Trader Who Needs to Systematize

You understand markets deeply but your edge is implicit, discretionary, and hard to scale. This course gives you the framework to convert market intuition into auditable, reproducible systems — without losing the structural understanding that makes the intuition valuable.

3
The Researcher Who Needs Execution Reality

You know how to build signals but you have not yet confronted what markets do to them once costs, liquidity, regime change, and systemic stress are allowed to play their full role. Pillar 3 alone will change how you read every backtest you have ever run.
